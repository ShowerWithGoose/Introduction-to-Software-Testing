Based on the comparison between the buggy output and the expected output, the program fails to output the `<Stmt>` tag for simple assignment statements (e.g., `a = a+x;`, `x = x+1;`) and other statement types that are not `if`, `for`, `break`, `continue`, `return`, or `printf`.

Looking at the `Parser.java` file, specifically the `handleComplexStmt` method:
1. It handles `LVal '=' Exp` (Assignment).
2. In the `else` branch (standard assignment), it creates a `MyAssignStmt` but **fails to call** `addSyntax(SyntaxType.Stmt)` before returning.
3. Similarly, for `LVal '=' 'getint'...` and `LVal '=' 'getchar'...`, it also misses the `addSyntax(SyntaxType.Stmt)` call.
4. The `Stmt` constructor for expressions (`new Stmt(exp)`) is used in the fallback case, which is correct, but the specific assignment handlers are missing the syntax tag addition.

Additionally, looking at `handleBreakOrContinue`, `handleReturnStmt`, and `handlePrintfStmt`, they correctly call `addSyntax(SyntaxType.Stmt)`. The `handleIfStmt` and `handleForStmt` also call it. The issue is isolated to `handleComplexStmt`.
