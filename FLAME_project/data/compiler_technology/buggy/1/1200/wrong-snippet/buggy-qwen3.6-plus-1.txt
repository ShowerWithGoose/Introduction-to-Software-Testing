Looking at the `Parser.java` file, specifically the `parseCompUnit` method:

1.  The logic attempts to distinguish between `Decl`, `FuncDef`, and `MainFuncDef` by looking ahead up to 3 tokens (`first`, `second`, `third`).
2.  For `int main() { ... }`:

    *   `second` is `MAINTK` ("main").
    *   `third` is `LPARENT` ("(").
    *   The condition `first.getType().equals(LexType.INTTK)` is true.
    *   The condition `third.getType().equals(LexType.LPARENT)` is true.
    *   The condition `second.getType().equals(LexType.MAINTK)` is true.
    *   It calls `parseMainFuncDef()`.

However, there is a critical issue in how `parseMainFuncDef` and other parsing methods interact with the lexer's state if an error occurs or if the lookahead logic in `parseCompUnit` consumes tokens incorrectly or fails to handle the end of the stream.

But the most likely cause for **empty output** when the expected output is substantial is that the `CompUnit` object or its children are not generating output correctly, OR the parser fails to enter the loop or breaks out prematurely.
