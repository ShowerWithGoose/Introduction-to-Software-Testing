    ERRORAND("&", false), // @@ [This pattern matches a single '&', but it appears before AND("&&", ...). Since the lexer iterates over TokenType.values() in declaration order, a single '&' will be matched by ERRORAND before the lexer has a chance to try matching "&&". However, the real issue is that the ERRORAND and ERROROR patterns are too broad—they match even when '&' or '|' are part of valid tokens like "&&" or "||", but because the valid tokens come later, they are never reached. The ordering is wrong: longer tokens like "&&" and "||" must be matched before single "&" or "|".]
