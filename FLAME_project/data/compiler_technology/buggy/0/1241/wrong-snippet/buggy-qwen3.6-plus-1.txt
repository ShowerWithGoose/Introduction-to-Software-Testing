The program outputs `STRCON ""` (an empty string) instead of `STRCON "21373119"` or `STRCON "%d"`. This indicates that the content inside the double quotes is not being captured correctly.

Looking at the `Lexer.java` file, specifically the handling of `isDoubleQuote(c)`:

The logic inside the `while(true)` loop reads the next character `c = line.charAt(++index)`. If it's a backslash, it appends the backslash and the next character. If it's a double quote, it appends it and breaks. However, **it fails to append regular characters** that are neither backslashes nor double quotes. It just loops around, reading the next character without adding the current one to `tokenValue`.
