### Summary of Issues
1. **Index Out of Bounds in Lookahead Checks**: In `Syntactic.java`, the methods `parseDecls()` and `parseFuncDefs()` use `getNext(2)` and `getNext(1)` to peek ahead in the token list without verifying that enough tokens remain. This will throw an `IndexOutOfBoundsException` when parsing near the end of the file or when the token list is short.
2. **Unsafe Boundary Check in `isLval()`**: The `isLval()` method scans forward until it finds `ASSIGN` or `SEMICN`. If it reaches the end of the token list without finding either, `curTmp` equals `words.size()`, and `words.get(curTmp)` throws an `IndexOutOfBoundsException`.
3. **State Mutation in `AddExp.syntaxOutput()`**: The `syntaxOutput()` method in `AddExp.java` removes elements from `mulExps` and `ops` to perform recursive output but **fails to restore them** afterward. This permanently alters the AST node's internal state, which can cause crashes or incorrect output if the method is invoked more than once or interferes with other traversal logic.
