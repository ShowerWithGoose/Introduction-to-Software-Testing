### Summary of Issues
1. **`Fault.java` `printFault` Method**: Contains hardcoded debugging conditions (`faultList.size() != 3`, checking exclusively for error code `'i'`, and digit filtering) that prevent the method from correctly outputting syntax errors for general test cases. This will cause the program to silently fail to report errors.
2. **`Parser.java` `compUnit` & `unaryExp` Methods**: Use unsafe lookahead logic. `getToken(index + n).getTokenType()` and `tokenList.get(index + 1).getTokenType()` will throw `NullPointerException` or `IndexOutOfBoundsException` when the parser reaches the end of the token list, as `Token.OUT_INDEX` has a `null` `tokenType` and direct list access lacks bounds checking.
3. **Grammar Parsing Logic**: The `compUnit` lookahead strategy (`index + 2` for `LPARENT`, `index + 1` for `MAINTK`) is fragile and can lead to infinite loops or incorrect parsing branches when encountering valid declarations or function definitions near EOF.
