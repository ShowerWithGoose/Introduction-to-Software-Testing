**Summary of the Issue:**
The lexical analyzer incorrectly handles the detection of individual `&` and `|` symbols (error type `a`). When the lexer encounters a single `&` or `|`, it correctly reads the next character to check for `&&` or `||`. However, if the next character does not match, the code reports the error but **fails to call `retract()`**. This causes the lexer to permanently consume and skip the character immediately following the invalid symbol, leading to incorrect tokenization or missed tokens in subsequent parsing steps. The `retract()` method must be added to the `else` branches to push the lookahead character back into the input stream.
