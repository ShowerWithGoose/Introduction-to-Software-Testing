### Summary of the Issue
The primary logical error lies in the **line number tracking mechanism** within `LexicalAnalyzer.java`. The `lineNum` variable is only incremented in the final `else` block of the `parse()` loop when a newline character (`\n`) falls through all token-matching conditions. However, newlines encountered in other contexts (e.g., inside the blank-skipping loop, or when `retract()` moves the cursor back across a line boundary) are not properly accounted for. Since the error reporting for `&` and `|` (error type `a`) relies on `lineNum`, this flawed tracking will result in incorrect line numbers being written to `error.txt`. Additionally, the commented-out line number updates in `getSym()` and `retract()` indicate an incomplete implementation of cross-line cursor management.
