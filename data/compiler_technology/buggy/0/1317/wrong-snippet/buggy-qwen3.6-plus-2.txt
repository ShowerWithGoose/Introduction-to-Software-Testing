**Summary of Errors:**
1. **Operator Precedence Bug in `parseIdent()`**: The `while` loop condition `pos < line.length() && isIdent(line.charAt(pos)) || isDigit(line.charAt(pos))` is evaluated as `(pos < line.length() && isIdent(line.charAt(pos))) || isDigit(line.charAt(pos))` due to `&&` having higher precedence than `||`. When `pos` reaches the end of the line, the first part evaluates to `false`, but `isDigit(line.charAt(pos))` is still evaluated, causing an `IndexOutOfBoundsException`.
2. **Missing `break` Statement in `parseSign()`**: The `case '/':` block lacks a `break;` statement. This causes execution to fall through to the `default:` case, incorrectly appending the `/` character again and attempting to generate a duplicate/invalid token, which corrupts the lexical output.
3. **Missing Bounds Checks in `parseSign()`**: Several `line.charAt(pos)` calls (e.g., when checking for `&&`, `||`, `!=`, `<=`, `//`, `/*`) do not verify `pos < line.length()` first. If these operators appear at the very end of a line, it will trigger a runtime `StringIndexOutOfBoundsException`.
