In Java's `BufferedInputStream`, passing `0` as the `readlimit` immediately invalidates the mark. Consequently, subsequent calls to `in.reset()` will throw an `IOException` or fail silently, breaking the keyword/identifier matching logic and causing the program to crash or misparse tokens.
2. **Character Consumption in Error/Comment Handling**: The handling of single `&`/`|` and `/* ... */` comments incorrectly consumes characters via `in.read()` without resetting the stream when a match fails, leading to lost input and subsequent token misalignment.
