Based on the problem description and code analysis, the primary logical error lies in the error recovery mechanisms within the parser. Specifically, in `ErrorHandler.java`, the methods `handleIError`, `handleJError`, and `handleKError` incorrectly call `tokens.stepBack(2)` immediately after `tokens.next()`. Since `next()` advances the token iterator by exactly one position, `stepBack(1)` is required to correctly revert to the previous parsing state. Using `stepBack(2)` causes the iterator to skip an additional token, leading to misaligned parsing states, incorrect error line reporting, and potential `IndexOutOfBoundsException` or infinite loops during syntax analysis. A similar `stepBack(2)` issue also exists in `MyNopStmt.java`.
