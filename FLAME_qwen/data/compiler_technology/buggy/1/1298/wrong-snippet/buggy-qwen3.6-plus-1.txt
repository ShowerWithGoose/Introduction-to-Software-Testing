### Summary of the Issue
The test case indicates a **Compilation Error**. Upon analyzing `frontend/GrammarAnalyse.java`, the compilation failure is caused by **name shadowing**. The class defines several parsing methods (e.g., `MulExp`, `AddExp`, `RelExp`, `EqExp`, `LAndExp`, `LOrExp`) that share the exact same names as the `Enums.V` constants statically imported at the top of the file. In Java, method names in the same scope shadow statically imported enum constants. Consequently, when these unqualified names are passed as the second argument to the `TreeNode` constructor, the compiler interprets them as method references rather than `Enums.V` values, resulting in a type mismatch compilation error. These identifiers must be explicitly qualified with `Enums.V.` to resolve the conflict.
