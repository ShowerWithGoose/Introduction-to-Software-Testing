However, looking at the syntax analysis implementation, there's a critical issue in the **ForStatement parsing**.

The main error is in the `ForStatement.parse()` method in `<fronted/parser/statement/ForStatement.java>`. The method assumes that after reading the 'for' token, the next token is always '(' and calls `list.next()` without checking if it actually is '('. This violates the grammar rule and doesn't handle missing parenthesis errors properly.

Additionally, the method doesn't validate tokens before consuming them with `list.next()`, which can lead to incorrect parsing or crashes when encountering malformed input.
