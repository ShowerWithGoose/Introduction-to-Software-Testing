### Summary of the Issue
The primary logical error in the lexical analyzer lies in the **missing boundary checks** when parsing tokens. Specifically:
1. The `while` loops for identifiers (`isalnum`) and integers (`isdigit`) do not verify `i < line.size()`. If a token ends at the very last character of a line, `i` will increment to `line.size()`, causing an out-of-bounds array access (`line[i]`) in the loop condition, leading to undefined behavior or runtime crashes.
2. The character constant parsing block accesses `line[i + 1]` without first checking if `i + 1 < line.size()`, which can also cause out-of-bounds access when the single quote is at the end of a line.
