**Summary**
The lexical analyzer relies on a dynamic list of active parsers (`curParsers`) to match tokens character-by-character. After a token is successfully recognized or an error is detected, `resetParsers()` is invoked to reinitialize the parser list for the next token. However, the current implementation of `resetParsers()` only pushes all parsers from `allParsers` into `curParsers` without clearing the vector first. This causes `curParsers` to accumulate duplicate parser pointers across multiple calls to `getNext()`. Consequently, `calcParseRes()` processes duplicate parsers, incorrectly inflating `totalCanUse` and `totalCanNext`, which breaks the lexical analysis logic after the first token is processed.
