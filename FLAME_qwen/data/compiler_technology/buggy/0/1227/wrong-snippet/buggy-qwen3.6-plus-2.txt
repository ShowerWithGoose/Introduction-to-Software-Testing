**Summary**
The provided lexical analyzer contains critical logical and semantic errors that will likely cause a `StringIndexOutOfBoundsException` at runtime. The primary issue is the repeated use of `text.charAt(ptr)` without verifying that `ptr < text.length()`. This occurs when peeking at the next character for two-symbol operators (like `&`, `|`, `=`, `!`, etc.) and during the scanning of identifiers, integers, and strings. Additionally, the stateful logic for handling `&` and `|` using `continue` and accumulating characters in `token` is overly complex and prone to incorrect tokenization or infinite loops when encountering edge cases (e.g., `&` at EOF, or malformed `&&` sequences).
