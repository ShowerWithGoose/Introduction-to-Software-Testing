**Summary of the Issue:**
The lexical analyzer fails to correctly track line numbers (`curLine`) when newline characters (`\n`) are consumed as part of whitespace skipping or inside comments (`//` and `/* */`). 
1. In the whitespace handling block, `Character.isWhitespace()` returns `true` for `\n`. When newlines appear alongside other whitespace (e.g., spaces or tabs), they are consumed by the `do-while` loop without incrementing `curLine`.
2. In the `//` comment handling block, the loop consumes the terminating `\n` but neither increments `curLine` nor unreads it, causing subsequent tokens to be assigned to the wrong line.
3. In the `/* */` comment handling block, newlines inside the block comment are consumed without updating `curLine`.

These issues lead to incorrect line numbers in both `lexer.txt` and `error.txt`.
