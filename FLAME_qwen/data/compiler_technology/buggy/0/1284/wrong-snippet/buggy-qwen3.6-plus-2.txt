### Summary of the Issue
The program crashes with a `NullPointerException` during lexical analysis when an error is encountered. In `ErrorHandling.java`, the `errors` list is declared as `static` but is only initialized inside the instance constructor `public ErrorHandling()`. Since `Compiler.java` never instantiates the `ErrorHandling` class, the static `errors` list remains `null`. When `Lexer.java` detects a lexical error (such as a standalone `&` or `|`) and calls `ErrorHandling.addError()`, it attempts to invoke `errors.add()`, which throws a `NullPointerException`. The `errors` list must be initialized statically at the point of declaration.
