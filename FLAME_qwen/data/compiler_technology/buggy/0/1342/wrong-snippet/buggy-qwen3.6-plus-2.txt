However, the conditional blocks responsible for handling `&` and `|` are missing `continue;` statements at the end of their execution paths. Without `continue;`, the control flow falls through to the subsequent `else if` conditions after processing the character. This can cause an `IndexOutOfBoundsException` (if the symbol is at the end of a line) or incorrectly re-evaluate the next character, leading to compilation/runtime failures or incorrect tokenization.
