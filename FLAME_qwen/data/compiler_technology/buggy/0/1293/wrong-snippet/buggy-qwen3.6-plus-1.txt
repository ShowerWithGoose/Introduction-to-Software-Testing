### Summary of the Issue
The program fails to handle boundary conditions when traversing the input string. Throughout the `next()` method in `frontend/Lexer.java`, `this.line.charAt(this.pos)` is called without first verifying that `this.pos < this.line.length()`. This occurs during:
1. **Digit parsing**: Accessing the next character immediately after incrementing `pos` without bounds checking.
2. **Multi-character operators & comments**: Checking for `<=`, `>=`, `==`, `!=`, `&&`, `||`, `//`, `/*` without ensuring `pos` is within bounds.
3. **String/Char literal parsing**: Looping until a closing quote is found without checking for end-of-file.
4. **Identifier parsing**: Similar out-of-bounds access when scanning identifier characters.

When a token appears at the end of a line or the file, these unchecked accesses throw a `StringIndexOutOfBoundsException`, causing the program to crash before producing any output. Additionally, the loop conditions for digit and identifier parsing are logically flawed, allowing `pos` to exceed valid indices before the crash occurs.
