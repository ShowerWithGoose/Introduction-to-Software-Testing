### Summary
The program implements a lexical analyzer that needs to detect error type `a` (individual `&` or `|` symbols). In the `handleAndorOr` method, when an individual `&` or `|` is encountered, the code correctly records the error but **fails to consume the erroneous character or reset `currentToken`**. Since `getsym()` returns `currentToken` (which may hold a stale token from the previous iteration or remain `null`), the main parsing loop in `Compiler.java` may terminate prematurely or output incorrect tokens. Additionally, both `handleAndorOr` and `handleDoubleDelim` misuse `reserver()` for operator lookup; while it functionally works due to `getNameString()` matching, it is semantically incorrect and fragile.
