### Summary of Identified Errors
Based on the grammar rules and the recursive descent parser implementation, there are two primary logical/semantic errors in `Parser.java`:

1. **Typo in `parseInitVal`**: When parsing array initialization values enclosed in `{ }`, the condition `isNotMatch(currToken, LexType.RBRACK)` incorrectly checks for a right square bracket `]` instead of a right brace `}`. This causes the parser to misinterpret the end of the initializer list, leading to parsing failures or incorrect tree construction for array variables.
2. **Missing Lookahead for `char` in `parseCompUnit`**: The loop conditions for distinguishing `Decl` (declarations) and `FuncDef` (function definitions) correctly check for `LPARENT` when the type is `int`, but fail to do so for `char`. The condition `isMatch(currToken, LexType.CHARTK)` alone will incorrectly route `char` variable declarations (e.g., `char a;`) into the function definition parser, causing syntax errors or misparsed trees.
