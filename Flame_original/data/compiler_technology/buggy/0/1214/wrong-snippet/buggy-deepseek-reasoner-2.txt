The issue is that after the series of `if-else if` branches for specific characters (like `+`, `-`, `*`, etc.), there is no `else` branch to handle unrecognized characters. When the lexer encounters a character that is not a blank, digit, letter, or any of the predefined operators/delimiters, it simply does nothing (`returns` without advancing `curPos` and without adding a token). This causes the parsing to stop prematurely, leading to empty or incomplete token lists. The program should either report a lexical error for such characters or skip them, but currently it fails silently.
