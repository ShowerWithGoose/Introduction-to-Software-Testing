### Summary
The lexical analyzer contains a critical logical flaw: it accesses `line.charAt(idx)` in multiple places without first verifying that `idx < line.length()`. When operators like `&`, `|`, `!`, `/`, `<`, `>`, or `=` appear at the end of a line, `idx` equals the string length, causing a `StringIndexOutOfBoundsException` at runtime. Specifically for error type `a` (individual `&` or `|`), the program crashes instead of safely detecting the missing second character and reporting the error to `error.txt`. The same bounds-checking issue exists in the integer parsing loop and other multi-character operator handlers.
