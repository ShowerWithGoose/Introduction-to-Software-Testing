### Summary of the Issue
The provided lexical analyzer reads the source file, processes it character by character, and generates tokens or reports lexical errors. However, there is a critical logical omission in the handling of the `/` character. When the lexer encounters `/`, it correctly identifies and skips `//` (single-line) and `/* */` (multi-line) comments, but **it completely lacks an `else` branch to handle `/` as a division operator (`DIV`)**. As a result, any standalone `/` used for division will be silently ignored, causing missing tokens in `lexer.txt` and potentially breaking subsequent parsing stages.
